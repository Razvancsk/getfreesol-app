A private swap (also called stealth swap, shielded swap, privacy-preserving swap) is a crypto token swap that hides some or all information about who is trading, what wallet is involved, or what route the trade took, compared to a normal public DEX swap.

Here’s the simplest way to understand it:

✅ Normal Swap (public)

On Solana or Ethereum, a normal swap is fully visible:

Your wallet → sends tokens

Public DEX pool → processes swap

You receive tokens

Anyone on a block explorer can see:

Your wallet address

What tokens you swapped

How much

Exact time

Which pools you used

This is how Jupiter, Raydium, Orca, Uniswap, etc. usually work.

🔒 Private Swap (privacy version)

A private swap hides some of that information, usually by mixing, routing, or shielding.

Depending on the protocol, a private swap can:

1. Hide your wallet address

Observers cannot easily tell which wallet performed the swap.

2. Hide trade amounts

Amounts might be encrypted or mixed to avoid direct identification.

3. Hide trading strategy

Bots cannot copy-trade, front-run, or analyze your behavior.

4. Break the link between “wallet A sent tokens” → “wallet A received tokens”

This is the most important part:
A private swap makes your input and output appear unconnected, even though they are related inside the protocol.

🧩 How private swaps usually work

Different protocols use different techniques. Most common:

A. Shielded Pools

Tokens enter a private contract that uses:

zero-knowledge proofs (ZK)

internal accounting
The blockchain only sees "deposit" and "withdraw," not the swap inside.

Example: Zcash shielded pool, Aztec, Penumbra.

B. Stealth Routing

Trades are routed through:

service-owned wallets

internal liquidity

hidden execution pathways

The chain only sees generic activity, not the exact path.

This is what Vanish Trade aims for.

C. Blending / mixing

Your deposit is mixed with many other users, making it impossible to know which output belongs to you.

🧠 Why people use private swaps

Not to hide crime (that’s illegal), but for legitimate privacy reasons:

Don’t want competitors to see your trading strategy

Don’t want wallets doxxed (identity linked to wallet)

Protect against front-running bots

Keep portfolio private

Avoid stalkers, scammers, or unwanted tracking

OTC or large size trades that need stealth execution

Privacy ≠ crime. It’s a normal need.
